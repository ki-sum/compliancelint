## Top 3 Non-Compliant findings (tomorrow's fix targets)

1. **Art. 4 / ART04-OBL-1 — AI literacy.** No formal AI literacy programme for staff or end-users is documented. README/CONTRIBUTING/user-guide describe *the product*, not a structured AI-literacy curriculum. **Fix:** add `docs/ai-literacy.md` (or section in CONTRIBUTING) covering: how Claude/GPT is used in scanning, known failure modes, when to override AI verdicts, and contributor onboarding for regulatory-content reviewers.
2. **Art. 80 / ART80-OBL-4 — Compliance remediation plan.** No documented remediation plan to bring AI systems into compliance when reclassified to high-risk. **Fix:** add a documented procedure (e.g. `docs/non-high-risk-reclassification.md`) describing the steps Kisum will take if Commission/AI-Office reclassifies ComplianceLint outputs.
3. **Art. 111 / ART111-OBL-1 — Annex X legacy transition plan.** No transition plan for AI systems already on the EU market before 2 Aug 2027. **Fix:** even though ComplianceLint is not currently an Annex X component, document the GPAI-deployer transition timeline (2 Aug 2025 / 2 Aug 2026 / 2 Aug 2027 / 31 Dec 2030) in CHANGELOG or a SECURITY-adjacent runbook.

## Top 3 Unable-to-Determine (scanner blind spots)

1. **Art. 6 / ART06-CLS-1 + ART06-CLS-2** — Risk-classification gates remain UTD-by-design even when the scanner has read the entire codebase. The current obligation forces "manual review recommended" regardless of evidence, which means the scanner can never auto-promote a self-evident limited-risk system. *Blind spot in our own scanner: Art 6 needs a "self-evidence" path when no Annex III/I markers are present anywhere.*
2. **Art. 51-55 / GPAI obligations** — Many UTDs because ComplianceLint is the **deployer** of a GPAI (Claude API) but not the **provider**. The current scope-gate doesn't differentiate "uses GPAI but is not GPAI provider" from "is GPAI provider", so OBL-1a..OBL-1d default to UTD. *Blind spot: scope-gate should fast-fail Art 51-55 when `is_gpai_provider=false`.*
3. **Art. 91-92 / Information-supply readiness** — Always UTD because the obligation requires evidence of "ability to supply information to Commission within 14 days", which can't be inferred from code alone. *Blind spot: this is a Human Gate by nature; should route to dashboard questionnaire automatically.*
